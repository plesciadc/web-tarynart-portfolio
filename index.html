    Geometric Figures / February 2016

Contrasting natural vs artifical

As humans have evolved, we have constantly strived to become more technologically advanced. We have emerged from a time of mostly natural objects to one that is more synthetic. Our natural landscapes have become scarcer, leaving us unsettled with what has become of our world. As a result, we have found ourselves yearning to mask the true artificial appearance of our objects through the use of colors, patterns, and illusions to make them appear more natural and aesthetically pleasing. Despite our efforts, we still find ourselves engulfed by the unnatural and industrial world that surrounds us.


I am pinpointing the contrast between natural and artificial things. I want to encourage the viewer to consider how synthetic objects have altered the world around them through the use of a vast array of forms and colors. I am focusing on forms that are unrealistic and abnormal to our natural world. I present these forms as imposters of nature, trying to fit in where they do not. My goal is to awaken the viewer to the idea that even though some objects may give the false reality of a natural appearance, many of those objects are being masked from their true synthetic form.
