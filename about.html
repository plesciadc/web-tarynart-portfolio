a few words about me

My name is Taryn Alyssa Schilling. I am a fourth year at the University of Cincinnati majoring in Fine Arts in the DAAP (Design, Architecture, Art, & Planning) program. I have loved art ever since I was a kid and by high school, knew I wanted to pursue a career that involved art. My favorite mediums to work with are oil paint, charcoal, and ceramic, but I love experimenting and trying out new mediums and techniques. The majority of my work I create for my family and friends. While I really enjoy giving my art away as gifts, I hope in the future that I will be able to sell some of my pieces.

Currently, I am unsure of what I want to do with my Fine Arts degree and where I want to take it. However, I recently thought about becoming an art teacher and studying Art Education. While this is still up in the air for me, I really like this idea because not only will I be able to teach kids different art mediums and techniques, I also will be able to share my knowledge of art with them and give them tips along the way. In addition, along side teaching children art processes and methods, I would also like to continue creating art in my spare time.

A little background about me… I am originally from Marietta, Ohio where I grew up with my mom, my dad, and my younger brother. I attended Warren Local High School, where I took advanced and AP courses, played softball for two years, and was a member of the Spanish Honor Society. In addition, I took 3 years of art classes and 1 year of advanced art during my senior year. I enjoyed going to games to support our teams, attending school dances and events, and hanging out with my friends as much as I could. Outside of school, I volunteered at my church, raised money for Relay for Life, and gave a hand at Help Me Grow, where my mom works. In addition, as soon as I turned 16, I got a job at McDonald’s where I worked 15-30 hours a week for 2 ½ years.
